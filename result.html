Student Results and Feedback: Impacting Lives

At the heart of our mental health-related website service for students are the experiences and feedback of the very individuals we aim to support. Here, we share some of the key results and insights gathered directly from our student users.

Mental Health Aspects

The top 3 challenges faced by the student community can be listed as:

  1. Academic Stress and Pressure:

    Challenges:
    Balancing coursework, assignments, exams, and academic expectations can lead to significant stress and anxiety among student.

    Resources:

    1. Academic Counseling:
    Meeting with academic advisors or counselors to discuss study strategies, time management, and academic planning.

    2. Stress Management Workshops:
    Participating in workshops or programs that teach stress reduction techniques and coping skills.

    3. Time Management Apps:
    Utilizing apps or tools to help organize schedules and prioritize tasks.

  2. Social Isolation and Loneliness:

    Challenges:
    Many students, especially those transitioning to a new academic environment, may experience feelings of isolation and loneliness, which can negatively impact mental health.

    Resources:

    1. Student Support Groups:
    Joining or forming student support groups or clubs focused on shared interests or hobbies to foster social connections.

    2. Counseling Services:
    Seeking individual or group therapy through campus counseling services to address feelings of isolation and loneliness.

    3. Online Forums and Communities: Engaging in online forums or communities where students can connect, share experiences, and provide support to one another..

  3. Mental Health Stigma and Barriers to Help-Seeking:

    Challenges:
    Stigma surrounding mental health issues can prevent students from seeking help or disclosing their struggles due to fear of judgment or discrimination.

    Resources:

    1. Campus Mental Health Centers:
    Accessing on-campus mental health services that provide confidential counseling and support.

    2. Online Mental Health Resources:
    Utilizing online platforms that offer anonymity, information, and self-help resources for mental health concerns.

    3. Awareness Campaigns and Workshops:
    Participating in mental health awareness campaigns and attending workshops that aim to reduce stigma and promote open dialogue about mental health.
